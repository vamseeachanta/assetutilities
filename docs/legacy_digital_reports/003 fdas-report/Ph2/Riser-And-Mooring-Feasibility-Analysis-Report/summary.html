2. Summary

Mooring and Riser analysis is performed to determine the feasiblity of the Frontier Deepwater Appraisal solution in 6000 ft water depth offshore Gulf of Mexico

2.1 Mooring Analysis

Mooring analysis is conducted using 8 point and 12 point moorings for 100 year and 1000 year hurricane conditions.

A 16 point mooring is anticiated to be required to help restrict the vessel offset to 4% offset in 1000 year return period hurricane condition.

2.2 Riser Analysis

Coupled riser analysis is conducted for 1000 year return period hurricane conditions.

The key analysis assumptions for the riser analysis are:

  • Coupled analysis is conducted using simulated load Response Amplitude Operators (RAOs) so as to take advantage of the decreased vessel motion due to riser stretch forces. Only 2 risers are assumed to be connected to the semi-submersible.
  • The semi-submersible vessel load RAOs is evaluated as described in Analysis Methodology section.
  • The tensioner design stroke range is assumed to be 40 ft with 10 ft allocated for upstroke and 30 ft for downstroke.

The key riser analysis findings are summarized below:

  • The riser tension range in 1000 year hurricane condition due to stretch and slack of riser is 1800 kips and 350 kips respectively.
  • With 2 risers connected, the max stretch stress (at 20 m below tension application point) is 80 ksi while the max slack stress (at bottom) is 60 ksi.
  • When 2 risers are connected to the semi-submersible in 1000 year hurricane conditions, the vessel heave range is decreased by 20% due to the enhanced riser stretch forces
  • The riser tension and thus the associated stress is expected to decrease further with 4 risers connected. However analysis is required to determine the riser response, vessel responses and airgap requirements for all possible configurations and loading conditions.
